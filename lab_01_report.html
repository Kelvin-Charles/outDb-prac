Step h) Count Records in Instructor, Teaches, and Section Tables

SQL Query:

SELECT 
    'instructor' AS Table_Name, 
    COUNT(*) AS Total_Records 
FROM instructor
UNION ALL
SELECT 
    'teaches' AS Table_Name, 
    COUNT(*) AS Total_Records 
FROM teaches
UNION ALL
SELECT 
    'section' AS Table_Name, 
    COUNT(*) AS Total_Records 
FROM section;
Figure 1h: Count of records in instructor, teaches, and section tables

Results:

  • instructor: 12 records
  • teaches: 15 records
  • section: 15 records

Result:

The query successfully counts and displays the total number of records in each of the three specified tables. This demonstrates the use of COUNT() function and UNION ALL to combine results from multiple tables.
